
About This Project

In this project, I analyzed Apple’s stock data to understand how the price changed over time and what insights we can get from it. I focused on trends, volatility, and how the stock behaved before and after 2020.

What I Wanted to Find
-Is Apple stock growing over time?
-What happened after 2020?
-Is the stock stable or risky?
-What do moving averages tell us?

Tools I Used
-Python
-Pandas
-Matplotlib
-Google Colab

About the Data

The dataset contains Apple’s historical stock data, including:

- Date
- Open, High, Low, Close
- Adjusted Close
- Volume


Data Preparation

First, I cleaned and prepared the data by:

Converting the date column
Sorting the data
Setting the date as index
Creating daily returns
Adding moving averages (MA50 and MA200)

What I Found

1. Stock Price Trend

The stock price increased a lot over time, especially in recent years. This shows strong long-term growth.

Before vs After 2020
Before 2020: around 7.7
After 2020: around 171

The increase is huge, which means the company grew significantly after 2020.

2. Volatility
   
Before 2020: higher
After 2020: lower

Even though the price increased, the stock became more stable.

3. Moving Averages

MA50 and MA200 helped show the trend clearly.
Most of the time, MA50 was above MA200, which indicates an upward trend.


Key Insights
Apple stock has strong long-term growth
The biggest change happened after 2020
The stock became more stable over time
The overall trend is upward

My Take

From this analysis, Apple looks like a strong long-term investment.
It not only grew a lot but also became more stable, which is important for investors.

Conclusion

Apple’s stock shows clear growth over time, especially after 2020.
At the same time, it became less volatile, which makes it interesting for long-term investors.
